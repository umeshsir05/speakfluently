Subject-Verb Agreement (संज्ञा-क्रिया सामंजस्य)

Subject-Verb Agreement means that the subject and verb in a sentence must agree in number (singular/plural) and person (first/second/third).

Basic Rule:
- Singular subject → Singular verb (adds -s/-es)
- Plural subject → Plural verb (base form)
He runs fast. (Singular)
They run fast. (Plural)

Important Rules of Subject-Verb Agreement

Rule 1: Singular vs. Plural Subjects

A singular subject takes a singular verb (usually ending in -s). A plural subject takes a plural verb (no -s).

Examples:
- The boy plays cricket. (Singular)
- The boys play cricket. (Plural)
- She writes a letter. / They write a letter.

Rule 2: Subjects joined by "and"

When two or more subjects are joined by and, use a plural verb.

Examples:
- Ram and Shyam are friends.
- Bread and butter are available.
Exception: If the two subjects refer to the same person/thing, use singular verb.
- The author and poet is coming. (same person)

Rule 3: Subjects joined by "or / nor / either...or / neither...nor"

The verb agrees with the subject closest to it (nearest subject rule).

Examples:
- Either Ram or his brothers have done it.
- Neither the students nor the teacher is present.
- Either Sita or I am going.

Rule 4: Indefinite Pronouns (everyone, someone, nobody, each, either, neither, etc.)

These pronouns are always singular and take singular verbs.

Examples:
- Everyone wants to succeed.
- Somebody has taken my pen.
- Nothing was done.
- Each of the boys is honest.
- Neither of the answers is correct.

Rule 5: Collective Nouns (team, family, committee, audience, etc.)

Collective nouns take a singular verb when the group acts as a single unit. They take a plural verb when the members act individually.

Examples:
- The team is playing well. (as a unit)
- The team are arguing among themselves. (individuals)
- My family is large. / The family have gone to different cities.

Rule 6: Uncountable Nouns (water, rice, information, furniture, etc.)

Uncountable nouns are always singular and take singular verbs.

Examples:
- Water is essential for life.
- The furniture looks beautiful.
- This information is useful.

Rule 7: Nouns that are plural in form but singular in meaning (Mathematics, Physics, News, etc.)

These take singular verbs.

Examples:
- Mathematics is my favorite subject.
- The news was shocking.
- Physics deals with matter and energy.

Rule 8: Subjects with "each" and "every"

When each or every comes before a compound subject, use a singular verb.

Examples:
- Each boy and girl has a pen.
- Every man, woman, and child was affected.

Rule 9: Expressions of quantity (a lot of, some, all, most, half, etc.)

Verb agrees with the noun after the preposition "of".

Examples:
- A lot of water has been wasted. (uncountable)
- A lot of students have passed. (countable plural)
- Some of the cake is left. / Some of the cakes are left.

Rule 10: There / Here sentences

The verb agrees with the subject that comes after "there" or "here".

Examples:
- There is a book on the table.
- There are many books on the table.
- Here comes the bus. / Here come the buses.

Rule 11: Titles, names, and quotes

Titles of books, movies, songs, etc. are treated as singular even if plural in form.

Examples:
- "The Lord of the Rings" is a famous trilogy.
- "Friends" was a popular TV show.

Rule 12: Relative Pronouns (who, which, that)

The verb in the relative clause agrees with the antecedent (the noun the pronoun refers to).

Examples:
- He is one of the boys who have done it. (antecedent: boys → plural)
- He is the only one of the boys who has done it. (antecedent: one → singular)

Rule 13: Money, time, distance, weight

These are treated as singular when considered as a single unit.

Examples:
- Fifty dollars is too much for this shirt.
- Three years is a long time.
- Ten kilometers is a long walk.

Rule 14: Gerunds as subjects

When a gerund (-ing form) or infinitive phrase acts as the subject, it takes a singular verb.

Examples:
- Running is good exercise.
- To err is human.
- Smoking causes cancer.

Rule 15: Inverted sentences (questions, "here/there")

Identify the true subject before choosing the verb.

Examples:
- Where are your friends? (subject: friends)
- Here comes the sun. (subject: sun)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Ignoring words between subject and verb (e.g., "The box of chocolates is [not are] empty.")
  • ❌ Using plural verb with "each/every/anyone/nobody"
  • ❌ Forgetting that "news, mathematics" are singular
  • ❌ Incorrectly matching verb with object of preposition instead of subject

Practice Exercises

Fill in the blanks with the correct form of the verb (answers below):

1. The list of items ______ (is/are) on the table.
2. Neither the teacher nor the students ______ (was/were) present.
3. Everyone ______ (want/wants) to be happy.
4. The team ______ (play/plays) their best every match.
5. Mathematics ______ (is/are) my favorite subject.
6. A lot of people ______ (has/have) attended the meeting.
7. There ______ (is/are) five books on the shelf.
8. Ten thousand rupees ______ (is/are) not a small amount.
9. Each of the girls ______ (has/have) a dress.
10. The news ______ (was/were) very sad.
Answers:
1. is 2. were 3. wants 4. plays 5. is 6. have 7. are 8. is 9. has 10. was
Remember: Always identify the true subject of the sentence. Ignore prepositional phrases that come between the subject and verb. Practice these rules regularly to avoid common errors in writing and speaking.
