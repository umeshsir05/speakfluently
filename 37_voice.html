Active and Passive voice



1. PRESENT TENSES
A. Simple Present-
Active Structure: Subject + V1/V1(s)
Passive Structure: Object + am/is/are + V3

Examples:
1. Active: She writes a letter.
Passive: A letter is written by her.
2. Active: They clean the room.
Passive: The room is cleaned by them.
3. Active: He plays cricket.
Passive: Cricket is played by him.

B. Present Continuous
Active Structure: Subject + am/is/are + V-ing
Passive Structure: Object + am/is/are + being + V3

Examples:
1. Active: She is cooking food.
Passive: Food is being cooked by her.
2. Active: They are watching TV.
Passive: TV is being watched by them.
3. Active: I am writing a story.
Passive: A story is being written by me.

C. Present Perfect-
Active Structure: Subject + has/have + V3
Passive Structure: Object + has/have + been + V3

Examples:
1. Active: She has completed the work.
Passive: The work has been completed by her.
2. Active: They have finished the homework.
Passive: The homework has been finished by them.
3. Active: He has written a book.
Passive: A book has been written by him.

D. Present Perfect Continuous
Active Structure: Subject + has/have + been + V-ing
Passive Structure: Not possible

Examples:
1. She has been learning English for two years.
2. They have been waiting for you.
3. I have been teaching since morning.

2. PAST TENSES
A. Simple Past-
Active Structure: Subject + V2
Passive Structure: Object + was/were + V3

Examples:
1. Active: He cleaned the room.
Passive: The room was cleaned by him.
2. Active: They helped the poor.
Passive: The poor were helped by them.
3. Active: She cooked food.
Passive: Food was cooked by her.

B. Past Continuous-
Active Structure: Subject + was/were + V-ing
Passive Structure: Object + was/were + being + V3

Examples:
1. Active: She was singing a song.
Passive: A song was being sung by her.
2. Active: They were repairing the car.
Passive: The car was being repaired by them.
3. Active: He was writing a letter.
Passive: A letter was being written by him.

C. Past Perfect-
Active Structure: Subject + had + V3
Passive Structure: Object + had + been + V3

Examples:
1. Active: She had completed the project.
Passive: The project had been completed by her.
2. Active: They had eaten the food.
Passive: The food had been eaten by them.
3. Active: He had repaired the bike.
Passive: The bike had been repaired by him.

D. Past Perfect Continuous
Active Structure: Subject + had + been + V-ing
Passive Structure: Not possible

Examples:
1. He had been studying for two hours.
2. They had been playing since morning.
3. She had been working all day.

3. FUTURE TENSES
A. Simple Future
Active Structure: Subject + will/shall + V1
Passive Structure: Object + will/shall + be + V3

Examples:
1. Active: He will finish the work.
Passive: The work will be finished by him.
2. Active: They will help you.
Passive: You will be helped by them.
3. Active: She will open the door.
Passive: The door will be opened by her.

B. Future Continuous
Active Structure: Subject + will/shall + be + V-ing
Passive Structure: Not possible

Examples:
1. He will be studying at 8 PM.
2. They will be traveling tomorrow.
3. She will be cooking lunch.

C. Future Perfect
Active Structure: Subject + will/shall + have + V3
Passive Structure: Object + will/shall + have + been + V3

Examples:
1. Active: She will have completed the assignment.
Passive: The assignment will have been completed by her.
2. Active: They will have built the house.
Passive: The house will have been built by them.
3. Active: He will have repaired the laptop.
Passive: The laptop will have been repaired by him.

D. Future Perfect Continuous
Active Structure: Subject + will/shall + have + been + V-ing
Passive Structure: Not possible

Examples:
1. He will have been working for five hours.
2. They will have been training since morning.
3. She will have been teaching for a year.
