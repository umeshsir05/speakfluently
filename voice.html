Active & Passive Voice (कर्तृवाच्य & कर्मवाच्य)

Voice is the form of a verb that indicates whether the subject performs the action (active) or receives the action (passive).

Examples:
- Active: Ram writes a letter. (Subject does action)
- Passive: A letter is written by Ram. (Subject receives action)

Rules for Converting Active to Passive

  • The object of the active sentence becomes the subject of the passive sentence.
  • The subject of the active sentence becomes the object of the passive sentence (often with "by").
  • The main verb is changed to its past participle (V3) form.
  • A suitable form of the verb "to be" (is, am, are, was, were, being, been) is added according to the tense.
  • Only transitive verbs (verbs that take an object) can be changed into passive.

Passive Voice with Modal Verbs

Structure: Modal + be + V3

Examples:
- Active: He can solve this problem.
- Passive: This problem can be solved by him.

- Active: You should obey your parents.
- Passive: Your parents should be obeyed by you.

- Active: She must finish the work.
- Passive: The work must be finished by her.

Passive Voice without "By"

Sometimes the agent (doer) is unknown, unimportant, or obvious, so it is omitted.

Examples:
- Active: Someone stole my wallet.
- Passive: My wallet was stolen. (by someone omitted)

- Active: People speak English all over the world.
- Passive: English is spoken all over the world.

Double Object Verbs

Some verbs take two objects (direct and indirect). Either object can become the subject in passive voice.

Example: She gave me a gift.
- Passive 1: I was given a gift by her.
- Passive 2: A gift was given to me by her.

Imperative Sentences (Commands/Requests)

Use "Let" + object + be + V3

Examples:
- Active: Open the door.
- Passive: Let the door be opened.

- Active: Please help me.
- Passive: You are requested to help me.

Interrogative Sentences (Questions)

Examples:
- Active: Did you complete the project?
- Passive: Was the project completed by you?

- Active: Who broke the window?
- Passive: By whom was the window broken?

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Intransitive verbs (e.g., sleep, go, happen) cannot be passive.
    He sleeps. (No passive)
  • ❌ Don't change the meaning or tense incorrectly.
  • ❌ Don't forget to use the correct form of "to be".
  • ✅ Use passive when the doer is unknown, unimportant, or you want to emphasize the action.

Practice Exercises

Change the following sentences from Active to Passive Voice (answers below):

1. The chef cooks delicious food.
2. She is painting a beautiful picture.
3. They have completed the project.
4. The teacher praised the student.
5. He will deliver the speech.
6. Someone has stolen my bicycle.
7. You must obey the rules.
8. Who wrote this book?
Answers:
1. Delicious food is cooked by the chef.
2. A beautiful picture is being painted by her.
3. The project has been completed by them.
4. The student was praised by the teacher.
5. The speech will be delivered by him.
6. My bicycle has been stolen. (by someone omitted)
7. The rules must be obeyed by you.
8. By whom was this book written?
Remember: Active voice is more direct and common in everyday speech. Passive voice is useful in formal writing, scientific reports, and when the doer is unknown or less important than the action.
