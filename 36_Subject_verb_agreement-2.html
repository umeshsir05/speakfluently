Subject Verb Agreement 2



Rule:
1.When two nouns are connected by along with, as well as, together with, like, besides, or not, the verb agrees with the first noun.
2.But, or से Connect होने पर verb second Noun के अनुसार use hoga.
Example :
1. The Principal along with the teachers is going on a trip.
(Verb “is” agrees with Principal.)
2. Not only the Principal but also the teachers are going on a trip.
(In “not only…but also”, the verb agrees with the noun closest to it → teachers, so “are”.)
Example
1.The house along with its contents has been sold out
2.One or two books is are still missing.
3.The boy not his parents is are guilty
4.Neither praise nor blame ..... to affect him.
(a) seem (b) seems (c) seeming (d) NOTA
5.Money besides fame require/ requires in life.

A number of [Many] → Plural verb
The number of + Singular
1. A number of books is/are missing from the library.
2. The number of students is/are increasing day by day.
3.Full many a flower are/is born to blush unseen
Many + Plural Noun + Plural verb
Many a + Singular Noun + Singular verb
4.Many a person has/have died in the accident.
5.Many persons has/have died in the accident,
6.Half of the apple is/are rotten.
7.Half of the apples is/are rotten.
