Subject Verb Agreement 2



Rule:
When two nouns are connected by along with, as well as, together with, like, besides, or not, the verb agrees with the first noun.
Example :
1. The Principal along with the teachers is going on a trip.
(Verb “is” agrees with Principal.)
2. Not only the Principal but also the teachers are going on a trip.
(In “not only…but also”, the verb agrees with the noun closest to it → teachers, so “are”.)
