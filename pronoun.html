Pronoun & Its Types (सर्वनाम और उसके प्रकार)

Pronoun is a word that is used in place of a noun to avoid repetition.

Example: Ram is a good boy. He studies hard.
(Here, 'He' is a pronoun used for 'Ram')

Types of Pronouns

1. Personal Pronoun (पुरुषवाचक सर्वनाम)

Personal pronouns refer to specific persons or things. They change form based on person, number, gender, and case.

PersonSingularPlural
1st Person (Speaker)I, me, my, minewe, us, our, ours
2nd Person (Listener)you, your, yours
3rd Person (Person/Thing spoken about)he, him, his, she, her, hers, it, itsthey, them, their, theirs
Examples: I am a teacher. / You are my friend. / He is playing. / She sings well. / It is a cat. / We are going. / They are happy.

2. Possessive Pronoun (अधिकारवाचक सर्वनाम)

Possessive pronouns show ownership or possession. They stand alone and are not followed by a noun.

Examples: This book is mine. / That car is yours. / The red bag is hers. / The house is ours. / The decision is theirs.

3. Reflexive Pronoun (निजवाचक सर्वनाम)

Reflexive pronouns are used when the subject and object of the sentence are the same. They end in -self (singular) or -selves (plural).

Examples: I hurt myself. / You should believe yourself. / He killed himself. / She looked at herself in the mirror. / They enjoyed themselves.

4. Demonstrative Pronoun (संकेतवाचक सर्वनाम)

Demonstrative pronouns point to specific persons or things. They are: this, that, these, those.

Examples: This is my pen. / That is a beautiful flower. / These are my friends. / Those are mountains.

5. Interrogative Pronoun (प्रश्नवाचक सर्वनाम)

Interrogative pronouns are used to ask questions. They are: who, whom, whose, which, what.

Examples: Who is at the door? / Whom did you meet? / Whose is this car? / Which is your favorite color? / What is your name?

6. Relative Pronoun (संबंधवाचक सर्वनाम)

Relative pronouns connect a clause or phrase to a noun or pronoun. They are: who, whom, whose, which, that.

Examples: The man who came here is my uncle. / The book which I bought is interesting. / This is the house that Jack built.

7. Indefinite Pronoun (अनिश्चितवाचक सर्वनाम)

Indefinite pronouns refer to non-specific persons or things. Examples: somebody, anyone, nobody, everyone, something, anything, nothing, everything, few, many, several, all, some, none, each, either, neither.

Examples: Somebody is knocking. / Everyone enjoyed the party. / Nothing is impossible. / Many were invited. / All is well.

8. Distributive Pronoun (विभागवाचक सर्वनाम)

Distributive pronouns refer to persons or things one at a time. They are: each, either, neither.

Examples: Each of the boys got a prize. / Either of the roads leads to the station. / Neither of them was guilty.

9. Reciprocal Pronoun (परस्परवाचक सर्वनाम)

Reciprocal pronouns express mutual action or relationship. They are: each other, one another.

Examples: They love each other. / The students helped one another.

Important Points:
- A pronoun must agree with its antecedent in number, gender, and person.
- Use subjective case (I, he, she, we, they) when the pronoun is the subject of a verb.
- Use objective case (me, him, her, us, them) when the pronoun is the object of a verb or preposition.
- Reflexive pronouns cannot be used as subjects (e.g., "Myself went" is incorrect).
- "Each other" is used for two persons; "one another" for more than two.
