📌 Modal Verbs (मोडल क्रियाएँ)

Modal verbs are helping verbs that express necessity, possibility, permission, ability, obligation, etc. They are always followed by the base form of the main verb.

Rules:
  • Modal verbs never change form (no -s, -ing, -ed).
  • They are followed by the base form of the main verb (without 'to').
  • To make negative, add 'not' after the modal.
  • To make questions, invert the modal and subject.

🔹 List of Modal Verbs with Examples

Can (सकता है / सकती है)
Ability, permission, possibility
I can swim.
मैं तैर सकता हूँ।
Can I go out?
क्या मैं बाहर जा सकता हूँ?
Could (सकता था)
Past ability, polite request, possibility
I could run fast when I was young.
जब मैं छोटा था तो तेज़ दौड़ सकता था।
Could you please help me?
क्या आप कृपया मेरी मदद कर सकते हैं?
May (सकता है / सकती है)
Permission, possibility (formal)
May I come in?
क्या मैं अंदर आ सकता हूँ?
It may rain today.
आज बारिश हो सकती है।
Might (शायद)
Less certain possibility
She might come to the party.
वह शायद पार्टी में आए।
Will (गा / गी / गे)
Future, intention, promise
I will call you tomorrow.
मैं तुम्हें कल फोन करूँगा।
Would (गा / गी / गे – past habit)
Past habit, polite request, conditional
He would visit us every Sunday.
वह हर रविवार हमसे मिलने आता था।
Would you like some tea?
क्या आप चाय पसंद करेंगे?
Shall (गा / गी – formal)
Future (I/we), suggestion, obligation
I shall return by 6 PM.
मैं शाम 6 बजे तक लौट आऊँगा।
Shall we go for a walk?
क्या हम टहलने चलें?
Should (चाहिए)
Advice, obligation, expectation
You should study regularly.
तुम्हें नियमित रूप से पढ़ना चाहिए।
Must (जरूर / चाहिए)
Strong obligation, necessity, certainty
You must obey the rules.
तुम्हें नियमों का पालन करना चाहिए।
It must be true.
यह सच होना चाहिए।
Ought to (चाहिए)
Moral obligation, advice
You ought to respect your elders.
तुम्हें अपने बड़ों का सम्मान करना चाहिए।
Used to (करता था)
Past habit, past state
I used to play cricket daily.
मैं रोज क्रिकेट खेलता था।
Need (ज़रूरत)
Necessity (mainly in negatives and questions)
You need not worry.
तुम्हें चिंता करने की ज़रूरत नहीं है।
Dare (हिम्मत)
Courage (mainly in negatives and questions)
He dare not speak the truth.
वह सच बोलने की हिम्मत नहीं करता।

📝 Negative and Interrogative Forms

Negative
Add 'not' after the modal
Cannot (can't), could not, may not, might not, will not (won't), would not (wouldn't), shall not (shan't), should not (shouldn't), must not (mustn't), ought not to, used not to, need not, dare not.
Interrogative
Invert the modal and subject
Can you swim? / Should I go? / May I come in?

🎯 Perfect Modals (Modal + have + V3)

Could have
Past possibility that did not happen
I could have helped you.
मैं तुम्हारी मदद कर सकता था।
May have / Might have
Past possibility
She may have forgotten.
वह भूल गई होगी।
Must have
Strong past certainty
He must have left already.
वह जा चुका होगा।
Should have
Past advice (not followed)
You should have studied harder.
तुम्हें और मेहनत से पढ़ना चाहिए था।
Would have
Past conditional
I would have come if I had known.
अगर मुझे पता होता तो मैं आ गया होता।
Note: Modal verbs do not take '-s' in the third person singular. They are followed by the base form of the verb (without 'to'). 'Ought to' and 'used to' are exceptions – they are followed by 'to'.

Practice modal verbs daily to express ability, possibility, and obligation correctly!
