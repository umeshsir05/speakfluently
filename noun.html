Noun & Its Types (संज्ञा और उसके प्रकार)

Noun is a word used as the name of a person, place, thing, animal, or idea.

Examples:
- Person: Rohan, teacher, doctor
- Place: Delhi, school, park
- Thing: table, phone, book
- Animal: dog, lion, elephant
- Idea: love, freedom, happiness

Types of Nouns

1. Proper Noun (व्यक्तिवाचक संज्ञा)

A proper noun is the name of a particular person, place, or thing. It always begins with a capital letter.

Examples: India, Mumbai, Amit, Taj Mahal, Monday, January

2. Common Noun (जातिवाचक संज्ञा)

A common noun is a name given in common to every person or thing of the same class or kind.

Examples: boy, girl, city, country, book, animal, teacher

3. Collective Noun (समूहवाचक संज्ञा)

A collective noun is the name of a group or collection of similar persons, animals, or things taken as one whole.

Examples: team, family, crowd, flock, herd, committee, army, bunch

4. Abstract Noun (भाववाचक संज्ञा)

An abstract noun is the name of a quality, action, or state that we can only think of or feel, not touch or see.

Examples: honesty, kindness, bravery, childhood, poverty, happiness, freedom

5. Concrete Noun (मूर्त संज्ञा)

A concrete noun is the name of something that can be seen, touched, tasted, heard, or smelled (perceived by senses).

Examples: apple, music, perfume, tree, car, phone, dog

6. Countable Noun (गणनीय संज्ञा)

Countable nouns are things that can be counted. They have both singular and plural forms.

Examples: one pen, two pens; a cat, many cats; an apple, five apples

7. Uncountable Noun (अगणनीय संज्ञा)

Uncountable nouns are things that cannot be counted. They do not usually have a plural form.

Examples: water, milk, sugar, rice, information, advice, furniture, money

Important Points:
- Proper nouns always start with a capital letter.
- Collective nouns, though referring to many, are often treated as singular (e.g., "The team is playing well").
- Abstract nouns cannot be seen or touched – they express ideas, qualities, or feelings.
- Uncountable nouns do not use "a/an" and take singular verbs (e.g., "Water is essential").
