Articles (A, An, The)

Articles are words that define a noun as specific or unspecific. English has three articles: A, An, The.

Examples:
- A cat (any cat)
- An apple (any apple)
- The sun (the only sun)

Types of Articles

1. Indefinite Articles: A / An

Used before singular countable nouns when the noun is not specific or is mentioned for the first time.

  • A is used before words that begin with a consonant sound.
    a boy, a university (yoo-ni-ver-si-ty), a one-eyed man (wun)
  • An is used before words that begin with a vowel sound (a, e, i, o, u).
    an apple, an hour (h is silent), an honest man, an MBA (em-bee-ay)
More Examples:
- I saw a bird. / She ate an orange.
- He is a European. (Eu sounds like "you")
- It is an honor to meet you.

2. Definite Article: The

Used before both singular and plural nouns when the noun is specific or already known to the listener/reader.

Examples:
- The sun rises in the east. (only one sun)
- I liked the book you gave me. (specific book)
- The rich should help the poor. (referring to a class)

Rules for Using Articles

✅ Use 'A/An' when:

  • Introducing something for the first time: I saw a dog.
  • Talking about someone's profession: She is a doctor.
  • Expressing a rate/price: ₹200 a kilo.
  • In exclamations with "What": What a beautiful day!

✅ Use 'The' when:

  • The noun is unique: the moon, the world
  • The noun has been mentioned before: I saw a bird. The bird was singing.
  • Superlatives and ordinals: the best, the first
  • Names of rivers, oceans, mountain ranges, deserts: the Ganges, the Himalayas
  • With adjectives to represent a class: the poor, the rich
  • With musical instruments: play the guitar
  • With countries that have plural names or include words like Republic, Kingdom: the United States, the UK

❌ Omission of Articles (No Article / Zero Article):

  • Before plural countable nouns used in general sense: Dogs are loyal. (not "the dogs")
  • Before uncountable nouns used generally: Water is life. (not "the water")
  • Before names of meals: I had breakfast. (not "a breakfast")
  • Before languages and subjects: English is easy. (not "the English")
  • Before most proper nouns (names of people, places): Ram, India
  • Before sports and games: She plays cricket.

Practice Exercises

Fill in the blanks with a, an, or the (answers below):
1. ______ sun rises in ______ east.
2. She wants to become ______ engineer.
3. I have ______ one-rupee coin.
4. He is ______ honest man.
5. ______ Ganga is ______ sacred river.
6. What ______ beautiful painting!
7. ______ rich should help ______ poor.
8. He plays ______ piano very well.
Answers:
1. The, the 2. an 3. a 4. an 5. The, a 6. a 7. The, the 8. the
Remember: The choice between 'a' and 'an' depends on the sound of the following word, not just the first letter. Use 'the' for specific references, unique objects, and superlatives. Use no article for general plural/uncountable nouns.
