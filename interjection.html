Interjection (विस्मयादिबोधक)

An interjection is a word or phrase that expresses sudden emotion, feeling, or reaction. It is often followed by an exclamation mark (!) and stands alone or at the beginning of a sentence.

Examples:
- Wow! That is amazing.
- Oh no! I forgot my keys.
- Hurray! We won the match.
- Alas! He is no more.

Common Interjections by Emotion

😊 Joy / Happiness (खुशी)

Hurray! Wow! Yay! Hooray! Great! Super! Fantastic!

Hurray! We have a holiday tomorrow.
Wow! What a beautiful painting.

😢 Sadness / Grief (दुःख)

Alas! Oh no! Ah! Ouch! Woe!

Alas! My pet dog died.
Oh no! I missed the bus.

😲 Surprise / Shock (आश्चर्य)

Oh! What! Hey! Gosh! My God! Good heavens!

Oh! I didn't see you there.
My God! Is that true?

😠 Anger / Frustration (गुस्सा)

Damn! What! Oh no! For heaven's sake! Blast!

Damn! I lost my phone.
What! You broke the vase?

🤔 Hesitation / Thinking (हिचकिचाहट)

Well! Um! Er! Hmm! Ah!

Well, I'm not sure about that.
Hmm, let me think.

👋 Greeting / Calling (अभिवादन)

Hello! Hi! Hey! Yo!

Hello! How are you?
Hey! Wait for me.

🙏 Relief / Gratitude (राहत / कृतज्ञता)

Phew! Thank God! Thanks! Whew!

Phew! That was a close call.
Thank God! You are safe.

🤐 Silence / Hushing (चुप कराना)

Shh! Hush! Quiet!

Shh! The baby is sleeping.

😣 Pain (दर्द)

Ouch! Ah! Ow! Eek!

Ouch! That hurts.
Ow! I stubbed my toe.

Punctuation Rules for Interjections

  • Strong interjections are followed by an exclamation mark (!).
    Wow! That's incredible.
  • Mild interjections are followed by a comma (,) or are part of the sentence.
    Well, let's see what happens.
    Oh, I didn't know that.
  • Interjections can stand alone as a sentence.
    Hurray! Alas!

Interjections vs. Other Parts of Speech

Some words can be interjections or other parts of speech depending on context.

WordAs InterjectionAs Other Part of Speech
WellWell! That's surprising.He speaks well (adverb).
OhOh! I see.-

HelloHello! How are you?Say hello to him (noun).

Practice Exercises

Fill in the blanks with suitable interjections from the box (answers below):
(Alas, Hurray, Oh, Ouch, Well, Shh, Wow, Hello)

1. ______ ! We won the championship.
2. ______ ! I have lost my wallet.
3. ______ ! That is a giant elephant.
4. ______ ! The needle pricked me.
5. ______ ! I didn't know you were here.
6. ______ , let's start the meeting.
7. ______ ! The baby is sleeping.
8. ______ ! How are you today?
Answers:
1. Hurray 2. Alas 3. Wow 4. Ouch 5. Oh 6. Well 7. Shh 8. Hello
Remember: Interjections are spontaneous expressions of emotion. They are not grammatically connected to the rest of the sentence. Use exclamation marks for strong feelings, commas for mild ones. In formal writing, use interjections sparingly.
