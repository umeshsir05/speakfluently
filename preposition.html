Preposition (पूर्वसर्ग)

A preposition is a word that shows the relationship between a noun (or pronoun) and another word in the sentence. It usually indicates position, direction, time, or manner.

Examples:
- The book is on the table. (position)
- She came after dinner. (time)
- He walked towards the door. (direction)

Types of Prepositions

1. Preposition of Place (स्थानवाचक)

Indicates where something is located.

PrepositionUsageExample
ininside a spaceHe is in the room.
onon a surfaceThe book is on the desk.
atspecific pointShe is waiting at the door.
underbelow somethingThe cat is under the chair.
abovehigher thanThe fan is above the bed.
betweenin the middle of twoHe sat between Ram and Shyam.
amongin the middle of manyShe is among friends.

2. Preposition of Time (समयवाचक)

Indicates when something happens.

PrepositionUsageExample
atclock time, festivalsMeet me at 5 PM.
inmonths, years, centuriesHe was born in 2000.
ondays, datesI will come on Monday.
sincestarting pointShe has been here since morning.
fordurationI waited for two hours.
duringwithin a periodHe slept during the class.
before/afterearlier/later thanCome before sunset.

3. Preposition of Direction/Movement (दिशावाचक)

Indicates movement from one place to another.

PrepositionUsageExample
tomovement towardsShe went to the market.
fromstarting pointHe came from Delhi.
intoenteringHe jumped into the pool.
out ofexitingShe walked out of the room.
towardsin the direction ofHe ran towards the bus.
throughgoing inside a passageThe train went through the tunnel.

4. Other Common Prepositions

  • of (belonging/relation): pages of the book
  • with (accompaniment): I went with my friend
  • without (lack): He left without saying goodbye
  • by (agent/means): written by Shakespeare, travel by train
  • for (purpose): This gift is for you
  • about (regarding): Tell me about yourself

Important Rules and Common Mistakes

✅ Prepositions with Time: In, At, On

  • At – specific time: at 7 o'clock, at noon, at night
  • In – longer periods: in the morning, in July, in 2025
  • On – specific days/dates: on Monday, on 15th August

✅ Prepositions with Place: In, At, On

  • In – inside a larger area (country, city, room): in India, in Mumbai, in the kitchen
  • At – specific point (address, shop, small place): at the door, at 5th Street
  • On – on a surface: on the table, on the wall

❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't use preposition before "home" (I am going to home) → I am going home.
  • Don't use "in" before last/following/next (He came in last week) → He came last week.
  • "Discuss" and "emphasize" take no preposition: Let's discuss about the matter → Let's discuss the matter.
  • "Arrive" is followed by "in" (city/country) or "at" (small place): arrive in Delhi, arrive at the airport.

List of Commonly Used Prepositions

One-word prepositions: about, above, across, after, against, among, around, at, before, behind, below, beneath, beside, between, beyond, by, down, during, except, for, from, in, inside, into, like, near, of, off, on, onto, over, since, through, to, toward, under, until, up, upon, with, within, without.

Compound prepositions (two or more words): according to, because of, in front of, in spite of, instead of, on behalf of, with regard to.

Practice Exercises

Fill in the blanks with the correct preposition (answers below):
1. The cat is hiding ______ the bed.
2. She has been waiting ______ 2 hours.
3. We will meet ______ Monday morning.
4. He lives ______ Delhi.
5. The train goes ______ the tunnel.
6. She is fond ______ music.
7. He apologized ______ his mistake.
8. The book was written ______ R.K. Narayan.
Answers:
1. under 2. for 3. on 4. in 5. through 6. of 7. for 8. by
Remember: Prepositions are often tricky because they don't always translate directly from Hindi. The best way to master them is to read and practice regularly. Notice how prepositions are used in sentences you read.
